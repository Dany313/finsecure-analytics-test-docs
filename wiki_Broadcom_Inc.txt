Source URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadcom_Inc.\n==================================================\n\nAmerican semiconductor company\nThis article is about the business formerly known as Avago, which was renamed to Broadcom after Avago took over the original Broadcom. For the original Broadcom before the 2016 takeover, see\nBroadcom Corporation\n.\n"Avago" redirects here. For the Irish/UK television channel, see\nAvago (TV channel)\n.\nBroadcom Inc.\nis an American\nmultinational\ndesigner, developer, manufacturer, and global supplier of a wide range of\nsemiconductor\nand infrastructure software products. Broadcom's product offerings serve the data center, networking, software, broadband, wireless, storage, and industrial markets. As of 2026—amid the\nAI boom\n—Broadcom is\none of the largest companies globally\n, and could be considered part of\nBig Tech\n.\nIn December 2024, Broadcom became the 12th company to surpass a $1 trillion\nmarket cap\n.\nIn April 2026, Broadcom surpassed a $2 trillion market cap,\nbecoming the sixth company in history to do so\n.\nAs of 2025, some 58% of Broadcom's revenue came from its semiconductor-based products and 42% from its infrastructure software products and services.\nTan Hock Eng\nis the company's\npresident\nand\nCEO\n.\nThe company is headquartered in\nPalo Alto, California\n.\nAvago Technologies Limited\nchanged its name to\nBroadcom\nto acquire\nBroadcom Corporation\nin January 2016. Avago's ticker symbol AVGO now represents the merged entity. The Broadcom Corporation ticker symbol BRCM was retired. Initially the merged entity was known as\nBroadcom Limited\n, before assuming the current name in November 2017.\nIn October 2019, the\nEuropean Union\nissued an interim antitrust order against Broadcom concerning anticompetitive business practices which allegedly violate\nEuropean Union competition law\n.\nIn May 2022, Broadcom announced an agreement to acquire\nVMware\nin a cash-and-stock transaction valued at $69\nbillion. The acquisition was closed on November 22, 2023.\nHistory\n[\nedit\n]\nOrigin in Hewlett-Packard\n[\nedit\n]\nThe company that would later become Broadcom Inc. was established in 1961 as\nHP Associates\n, a semiconductor products division of\nHewlett-Packard\n.\nThe division separated from Hewlett-Packard as part of the\nAgilent Technologies\nspinoff in 1999.\nFormation of Avago Technologies\n[\nedit\n]\nLogo of Avago Technologies\nKKR\nand\nSilver Lake Partners\nacquired the Semiconductor Products Group of Agilent Technologies in 2005 for $2.6 billion and formed Avago Technologies.\nAvago Technologies agreed to sell its I/O solutions unit to\nPMC-Sierra\nfor $42.5 million in October 2005.\nIn August 2008, the company filed an initial public offering of $400 million.\nIn October 2008, Avago Technologies acquired\nInfineon Technologies'\nMunich-based bulk acoustic wave business for €21.5 million.\nOn 6 August 2009, Avago Technologies went public on\nNASDAQ\nwith the ticker symbol AVGO.\nAvago chip inside an iPhone 5s\nAvago Technologies announced its agreement to acquire CyOptics, an optical chip and component supplier, for $400 million in April 2013.\nThe acquisition aimed to expand Avago Technologies' fiber optics product portfolio.\nIn October 2013, Avago Technologies invested $5 million in Amantys, a\npower electronics\ntechnology provider, as part of a strategic investment agreement between the two companies.\nAvago Technologies announced its agreement to acquire\nLSI Corporation\nin December 2013 for $6.6 billion.\nThe acquisition helped move Avago Technologies away from specialized products and towards a more mainstream industry, which included chips, especially storage for data centers.\nThe company sold its\nSSD controller\nbusiness to\nSeagate Technology\nin May 2014.\nIn August 2014, the company was the ninth-largest semiconductor company.\nAvago Technologies agreed to sell LSI's Axxia Networking business to\nIntel\nfor $650\nmillion.\nThe company also agreed to buy\nPLX Technology\n, an integrated circuits designer, for $309 million.\nIn February 2015, it was announced that Avago Technologies Limited had reached an agreement to acquire\nEmulex\nCorporation for $8 per share in cash.\nAcquisition of and change of name to Broadcom Limited\n[\nedit\n]\nBroadcom facility in Bangalore, India, as seen in 2019\nOn 28 May 2015, Avago announced that it would buy\nBroadcom Corporation\nfor $37 billion ($17 billion cash and $20 billion in shares).\nAvago was the nominal survivor, but the combined company would be named Broadcom Ltd. It would have annual revenue of $15 billion and a market value of $77 billion.\nBroadcom Corp. strengthened Avago Technologies' patent position significantly in sectors such as mobile, the data center, and the Internet of Things and made the company the ninth largest holder of patents among the top semiconductor vendors, according to an analysis by technology consulting firm\nLexInnova\n.\nIn May 2016,\nCypress Semiconductor\nannounced that it acquired Broadcom Corporation's full portfolio of\nIoT\nproducts for $550 million. Under the deal, Cypress acquired Broadcom's IoT products and intellectual property for Wi-Fi, Bluetooth and\nZigbee\nconnectivity, as well as Broadcom's WICED platform and SDK for developers. The deal combined Broadcom's developer tools and connectivity technologies for IoT devices with Cypress' own programmable\nsystem-on-a-chip\n(SoC) products that provide memory, computing and graphics processing for low-power devices.\nForeign investment reviews and change of name to Broadcom Inc.\n[\nedit\n]\nIn 2016, Broadcom proposed merging with\nBrocade Communications Systems\n. The acquisition proposal included a $5.5 billion price and was offered in cash.\nIt was delayed for review by the\nCommittee on Foreign Investment in the United States\n. On 2 November 2017, Broadcom announced it would relocate its legal address from Singapore to Delaware, which would avoid the review.\nThis action was linked to the parent company being renamed from Broadcom Limited to Broadcom Inc.\nThe pre-2016-merger Broadcom,\nBroadcom Corporation\n, remains a wholly owned subsidiary of the renamed parent Broadcom Inc.\nIn mid-November 2017, Broadcom proposed to purchase\nQualcomm\nfor US$130 billion, which was rebuffed by Qualcomm's board.\nThe proposed hostile takeover, which was later revised to $117 billion, was blocked by\nthe Trump administration\nby an\nexecutive order\nthat cited national security concerns.\nSpecifically, the\nCommittee on Foreign Investment in the United States\nexpressed concerns. Broadcom, then headquartered in Singapore, was considered too close to China and chipmaker\nHuawei\n.\n"A shift to Chinese dominance in 5G would have substantial negative national security consequences for the United States," CFIUS said. "While the United States remains dominant in the standards-setting space currently, China would likely compete robustly to fill any void left by Qualcomm as a result of this hostile takeover."\nHowever, critics of the move stated that the decision was motivated by competitiveness more than security concerns.\nBroadcom withdrew its takeover bid two days after the executive order.\nObservers have stated that President Trump's decision was as consistent with the balance of trade objectives as it was with security concerns.\nMove into software\n[\nedit\n]\nHistorically a semiconductor-based-only company,\nthe failure of the Qualcomm bid led Broadcom and its CEO to look at acquiring infrastructure software as an alternative way of growing in size.\nOn 11 July 2018, news sources reported that Broadcom and\nCA Technologies\nagreed on terms for an $18.9 billion acquisition.\nCA Technologies, formerly known as Computer Associates, was a longtime giant in software for\nmainframe computers\nthat had expanded its offerings into software for\ncloud computing\n.\nAnd on 5 November 2018, Broadcom announced that it had completed the acquisition of CA Technologies.\nOn 9 August 2019, Broadcom acquired the enterprise security business of\nSymantec Corporation\n(the consumer software portion of which is now known as\nGen Digital\n) for $10.7 billion in cash.\nThe deal continued Broadcom's push into software critical for corporate infrastructure.\nAnd on 4 November 2019, Broadcom announced that it had completed the acquisition of the business, as well as the Symantec name and brand.\nIn 2019, Broadcom was the fifth best performing\nstock\nof the 2010s, with a total return of 1,956%.\nAnti-competitive practices investigations\n[\nedit\n]\nIn January 2018, it was reported that the FTC had been investigating Broadcom for several months for engaging in anti-competitive tactics while negotiating with customers.\nIn 2021, Broadcom agreed to settle the antitrust complaint, in which the\nU.S. Federal Trade Commission\nclaimed that the company abused its monopoly power using restrictive contract terms and threats of retaliation against customers the company deemed "disloyal."\nIn October 2019, Broadcom was ordered by the\nEuropean Commission\nto stop allegedly anticompetitive practices.\nIn 2021, Broadcom agreed to settle the antitrust complaint which claimed it had abused its monopoly power through restrictive contract terms and threats of retaliation against non-compliant customers.\nSuch contract terms are alleged to stifle innovation and harm competition in the global supply market.\nEuropean Competition Commissioner Margrethe Vestager said that Broadcom's contract terms with six main customers would "create serious and irreversible harm to competition" if no action were taken.\nThe  company agreed to a commitment to suspend agreements containing exclusivity or quasi-exclusivity arrangements and a commitment not to enter into such agreements for seven years.\nFollowing its $69 billion acquisition of VMware in 2023, Broadcom faced renewed European regulatory scrutiny over its licensing of VMware products.\nIn March 2026, the Cloud Infrastructure Service Providers in Europe (CISPE) trade group filed a formal complaint with the\nEuropean Commission\n, alleging that Broadcom's licensing of VMware products to cloud service providers infringed EU antitrust rules and requesting interim measures to halt the closure of VMware's cloud service provider programme. CISPE alleged that since the acquisition, prices had risen tenfold, with payment demanded upfront and products bundled regardless of customer need. The European Commission confirmed it was assessing the complaint. Broadcom denied the allegations, stating CISPE was "an organisation funded by hyperscalers, which misrepresent the realities of the market".\nPatent suits\n[\nedit\n]\nIn 2017, Broadcom filed patent suits against smart TV manufacturers. The U.S. International Trade Commission ruled in favor of the smart TV manufacturers.\nIn 2020, Broadcom sued Netflix over multiple patent infringements.\nCritics have argued that Broadcom is suing Netflix for being more successful,\nciting the declining number of traditional pay television subscribers due to the rise of streaming services.\nThe Leichtman Research Group calculated that the largest pay TV providers in the U.S. – representing about 95% of the market – lost about 4,915,000 net video subscribers in 2019.\nRegular customers each pay $231 a year for their boxes, totaling almost $20 billion per year in profit for the cable industry.\n2020s\n[\nedit\n]\nBroadcom office in Cambridge Science Park, April 2011.\nOn January 7, 2020,\nAccenture PLC\nagreed to acquire Symantec's 300-person cybersecurity services division from Broadcom.\nIn February 2020, Broadcom announced the world's first\nWiFi 6E\nclient device, the BCM4389.\nIn early 2020,\nRaspberry Pi\nInc. revealed that their new boards would have a Broadcom BCM2711 chip.\nIn May 2022, Broadcom announced its deal to acquire the virtualization and cloud computing software vendor\nVMware\nfor $61 billion in a combination of cash and stock, with Broadcom assuming $8 billion in VMware debt.\nIn November 2022, the UK's\nCompetition and Markets Authority\nregulator announced it would investigate whether the Broadcom Inc. acquisition of VMware Inc. would "result in a substantial lessening of competition within any market or markets in the United Kingdom for goods or services".\nThe\nEuropean Commission\nalso said it was investigating the proposed acquisition, and as a result, Broadcom and VMware extended the planned completion date out to May 26, 2023.\nOn May 19, 2023, as both UK and EU regulators had yet to complete their investigations, the completion date was formally extended\nto August 26, 2023, and was later further extended to as late as November 26, 2023.\nOn November 21, 2023, Broadcom Inc. announced its plan to finalize the $69 billion acquisition of VMware Inc. on the following Wednesday. This acquisition, one of the largest in the technology sector, received global regulatory scrutiny, including from China, which approved the deal with specific conditions. These conditions require VMware's server software to be compatible with local hardware and not restrict customers to using Broadcom's hardware products. Despite concerns over rising\nChina-U.S.\ntensions, the approval from China, which was the last regulatory hurdle, allowed the deal to proceed.\nThe transaction closed on November 22, 2023.\nOn completion, CEO Hock Tan announced that the company would relocate its headquarters in San Jose to the VMware campus in\nPalo Alto, California\n.\nIn February 2024, Broadcom sold the end-user computing division, which it inherited from the VMware acquisition, to KKR for $4\nbillion.\nCorporate affairs\n[\nedit\n]\nFinances\n[\nedit\n]\nFor the fiscal year 2023, Broadcom reported earnings of US$14.08 billion, with an annual revenue of US$35.8 billion, an increase of 7.8% over the previous fiscal cycle.\nOwnership\n[\nedit\n]\nThe 10 largest shareholders of Broadcom in early 2024 were:\nThe Vanguard Group\n(10.03%)\nBlackRock\n(7.63%)\nCapital World Investors\n(4.53%)\nCapital International Investors\n(4.04%)\nState Street Corporation\n(3.95%)\nGeode Capital Management\n(2.12%)\nInsiders (2.04%)\nCapital Research Global Investors\n(1.98%)\nFidelity Investments\n(1.94%)\nMorgan Stanley\n(1.52%)\nBank of America\n(1.46%)\nProducts\n[\nedit\n]\nBroadcom sells a range of semiconductor and infrastructure software applications that serve the data center (mainframes), networking, software, broadband, wireless, and storage and industrial markets. Broadcom divulged that it is bringing two variants of the Tomahawk-3 to market. The first can provide up to\n12.8\nTbit/s\ntotal chip bandwidth and contains 256 integrated SerDes, supporting 32 ports at\n400\nGbit/s\n, 64 ports at\n200\nGbit/s\n, and 128 ports at\n100\nGbit/s\n. The second variant of the Tomahawk-3 has 160 of the 256 SerDes available and delivers\n8\nTbit/s\nof aggregate bandwidth. Broadcom is suggesting 80 ports at\n100\nGbit/s\n; or 48 ports at\n100\nGbit/s\nplus either 8 ports at\n400\nGbit/s\nor 16 ports at\n200\nGbit/s\n; or 96 ports at\n50\nGbit/s\nplus either 8 ports at\n400\nGbit/s\nor 16 ports at\n200\nGbit/s\n.\nThe Tomahawk 4 reached a speed of\n25.6\nTbit/s\nwhile the Tomahawk 5 has a maximum speed of\n51.2\nTbit/s\n.\nSoftware\n[\nedit\n]\nBroadcom has expanded from its original hardware base and also offers\nenterprise software\nproducts.\nSymantec Enterprise Security\n[\nedit\n]\nSee also:\nSymantec Endpoint Protection\nBroadcom operates its enterprise security business under the Symantec brand.
