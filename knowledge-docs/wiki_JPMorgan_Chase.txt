It is\nthe largest bank in the United States\n, and the world's\nlargest bank by market capitalization\nas of 2026.\nAs the largest of the\nBig Four\nbanks in America, the firm is considered\nsystemically important\nby the\nFinancial Stability Board\n. Its size and scale have often led to enhanced regulatory oversight as well as the development of an internal "Fortress Balance Sheet".\nJPMorgan Chase is located at\n270 Park Avenue\nin\nMidtown Manhattan\n. It was created in 2000 by the merger of New York City banks\nJ.P. Morgan & Co.\nand\nChase Manhattan Company\n. Through its predecessors, the firm's early history can be traced to 1799, with the founding of what became the Bank of the Manhattan Company. J.P. Morgan & Co. was founded in 1871 by the American financier\nJ. P. Morgan\n, who launched the\nHouse of Morgan\non\n23 Wall Street\nas a national purveyor of commercial, investment, and private banking services. Today, the firm is a major provider of investment banking services, through corporate advisory,\nmergers and acquisitions\n, sales and trading, and public offerings. Its private banking franchise and asset management division are among\nthe world's largest\nin terms of total assets. Its retail banking and credit card offerings are provided via the\nChase\nbrand worldwide.\nJPMorgan Chase is the\nworld's fifth-largest bank by total assets\n, with $\n4\ntrillion\nin assets as of 2026.\nThe firm operates the\nlargest investment bank\nin the world by revenue.\nIt occupies the 11th spot on the\nFortune\n500\nlist of the largest U.S. corporations by revenue.
