Source URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Novo_Nordisk\n==================================================\n\nDanish pharmaceutical company\nNovo Nordisk A/S\nis a\nDanish\nmultinational\npharmaceutical company\nheadquartered in\nBagsværd\n.\nNovo Nordisk is controlled by majority shareholder\nNovo Holdings A/S\n(wholly owned by the\nNovo Nordisk Foundation\n) which holds approximately 28.1% of its shares and a\nmajority\n(77.1%) of its voting shares.\nNovo Nordisk manufactures and markets\npharmaceutical\nproducts and services, specifically\ndiabetes\nand\nobesity\nmedications and devices.\nNovo Nordisk employs more than 48,000 people globally, and markets its products in 168 countries.\nThe corporation was created in 1989, through a merger of two Danish companies, which date back to the 1920s. Novo Nordisk is a full member of the\nEuropean Federation of Pharmaceutical Industries and Associations\n(EFPIA).\nThe company was ranked 25th among\nFortune\n's\n100 Best Companies to Work For\nin 2010, and subsequently ranked 72nd in 2014 and 73rd in 2017.\nIn January 2012, Novo Nordisk was named the most sustainable company in the world by the business magazine\nCorporate Knights\n,\nwhile spin-off company\nNovozymes\nwas named fourth.\nIt is a leader in the\nFTSE4Good Index\n, and the only European company in the top ten.\nNovo Nordisk is the largest pharmaceutical company in Denmark.\nNovo Nordisk's\nmarket capitalization\nexceeded the GDP of Denmark's\ndomestic economy\nin 2023, and it is the highest valued company in Europe.\nNet sales in 2024 were US$42.121 billion.\nHistory\n[\nedit\n]\n1922-1925\n[\nedit\n]\nIn 1922,\nAugust Krogh\n, a professor at the\nUniversity of Copenhagen\n, went on a lecture tour to North America after receiving the\nNobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine\n. During this tour, Krogh and his wife\nMarie\nvisited Toronto where the scientists\nFrederick Banting\n,\nCharles Best\nand\nJohn Macleod\nhad just succeeded in manufacturing active insulin. Krogh received permission to manufacture insulin in the Nordic countries and joined forces with\nHans Christian Hagedorn\n, a physician specialising in diabetes, to start the production of insulin in Denmark. This led to the establishment of Nordisk Insulinlaboratorium company in 1923.\nIn 1925, brothers\nHarald\nand\nThorvald Pedersen\n, who were former employees of Nordisk, formed their own company, Novo Terapeutisk Laboratorium. Novo and Nordisk competed until they merged in 1989 to become Novo Nordisk A/S.\n1982–1994\n[\nedit\n]\nThe company established its presence in the US in 1982 via a joint venture with\nSquibb Corporation\nand Canada in 1984.\nIn 1985, the company introduced the first insulin pen device called Novopen.\nIn 1989, Novo Industri A/S (Novo Terapeutisk Laboratorium) and Nordisk Gentofte A/S (Nordisk Insulinlaboratorium) merged to become Novo Nordisk A/S, the world's largest producer of insulin with headquarters in Bagsværd,\nCopenhagen\n.\nIn 1991, Novo Nordisk Engineering (now NNE A/S) demerged after working as in-house consultants at Novo Nordisk for years, to provide standard engineering services (end-to-end engineering) to pharma manufacturing companies.\nIn 1994, Novo Nordisk's existing information technology units was spun out as\nNNIT A/S\n. The company was converted into a wholly owned\naktieselskab\nin 2004\nIn March 2015, NNIT was floated on the\nNasdaq Nordic\n.\n2000–2018\n[\nedit\n]\nNovo's enzymes business,\nNovozymes A/S\n, was spun-out in 2000.\nNovo acquired\nXellia\nfor $700 million in 2013.\nThe same year, Novo Nordisk USA moved into new headquarters offices in\nPlainsboro Township, New Jersey\n, by way of extensively renovating abandoned premises. This action served to consolidate several facilities that the company had previously had in Plainsboro.\nIn 2015, the company announced it would collaborate with\nAblynx\n, using its nanobody technology to develop at least one new drug candidate.\nIn January 2018,\nReuters\nreported that Novo had offered to acquire Ablynx for $3.1 billion - having made an unreported offer in mid-December for the company.\nHowever, the Ablynx board rejected this offer the same day, explaining that the price undervalued the business.\nUltimately  Novo lost out to\nSanofi\nwho bid $4.8 billion. Later, in the same year, the company announced it would acquire Ziylo for around $800 million.\n2020–present\n[\nedit\n]\nIn March 2020, Novo volunteers started testing samples for\nSARS-CoV-2\nwith\nRT-qPCR\nequipment in the ongoing\ncoronavirus pandemic\nto increase available test capacity.\nIn June, the business announced it would acquire\nAstraZeneca\n's\ncardiovascular disease\n-focused spin-off Corvidia Therapeutics for an initial sum of $725 million (up to a performance-related maximum of $2.1 billion).\nIn November, the company announced it would acquire Emisphere Technologies for $1.8 billion, gaining control of a pill-based treatment for diabetes.\nNovo then announced in December that it would acquire Emisphere Technologies for $1.35 billion.\nIn November 2021, Novo announced it would acquire Dicerna Pharmaceuticals and its\nRNAi\ntherapeutics, for $3.3 billion ($38.25 per share).\nIn September 2022, Novo agreed to acquire Forma Therapeutics for $1.1 billion with the intent to expand its sickle cell disease and rare blood disorders portfolio.\nBy 2022 the popularity of Novo's\nWegovy\nand\nOzempic\nfor weight loss was so great as to significantly increase the growth of the entire\neconomy of Denmark\n. Two-thirds of Denmark's overall economic growth in 2022 was attributed to the pharmaceutical industry.\nThe company's profits increased by 45% year over year in the first half of 2023.\nMost of the growth occurred from its weight loss drugs, Wegovy and Ozempic, which accounted for 55% of the company's 2023 revenue.\nIn August 2023, Novo agreed to acquire the\nMontreal\n-headquartered pharmaceutical company, Inversago Pharma for $1 billion\nand Embark Biotech for up to $500 million.\nIn October 2023, the company announced it would acquire ocedurenone—an experimental drug for uncontrolled hypertension and potentially beneficial in treating cardiovascular and kidney diseases—from KBP Biosciences for $1.3\nbillion.\nAfter a failed clinical trial the following year, Novo Nordisk initiated legal action against KBP alleging that the company misrepresented the drug's effectiveness by concealing unfavorable clinical trial data. Seeking up to $830 million in damages, the\nSingapore International Commercial Court\ngranted Novo Nordisk's request for a freeze on KBP's assets and those of its founder, Huang Zhenhua.\nIn November 2023, Novo Nordisk announced investment of €2.1 billion in a French production facility to increase the production capacity and manufacturing of its popular anti-obesity medication.\nIn February 2024, parent company\nNovo Holdings A/S\nagreed to acquire\nCatalent\nfor $16.5\nbillion. On completion, Novo Nordisk said it would acquire three manufacturing facilities from its parent for $11\nbillion to scale up production to meet the massive demand for Wegovy and Ozempic.\nIn March 2024, Novo Nordisk reached a $604 billion market capitalization and became the 12th most valuable company in the world. The company's stock jumped to a record high after early trial data showed positive results for its new experimental weight loss pill\namycretin\n.\nThe company also announced it would acquire Cardior Pharmaceuticals and its cardiovascular disease portfolio for up to $1.1 billion.\nAs of April 2024, the flow of cash from Novo Nordisk's weight-loss drugs was continuing to solidify its status as the most valuable company in Europe, to the point that economists were worried that Denmark might come down with\nDutch disease\n(that is, a country that does only one thing well and nothing else).\nThe company's market capitalization of $570 billion remained larger than the entire economy of Denmark, its $2.3 billion income tax bill for 2023 made it the largest taxpayer in the country, and its rapid growth was driving nearly all of the expansion of Denmark's economy.\nThe company had started to move away from its traditional focus on diabetes care towards a more ambitious mission to "defeat serious chronic diseases", and towards that end, hired over 10,000 people in 2023 alone.\nTo effectively manage the rapid expansion of its workforce while maintaining its traditional corporate culture, the Novo Nordisk Way, the company put over 400 senior executives through a\nleadership development\nprogram called NNX, which stands for Novo Nordisk Next.\nIn May 2024, the company announced it would acquire Austrian fluid management service business, Single Use Support.\nIn June 2024, the company announced plans to build a new production plant in\nClayton, North Carolina\n, at a cost of $4.1 billion. It will be the company's fourth in the state of\nNorth Carolina\nand used for production of\nsemaglutide\nproducts Ozempic and Wegovy.\nThe company also announced plans to acquire three US-based Catalent sites in to increase production supply.\nAs of October 2024, Novo Nordisk was the second most valuable drug company in the world by market capitalization, second only to its competitor\nEli Lilly and Company\n.\nBy July 2025, Novo Nordisk had fallen to become the fifth most valuable drug company amid rising competition from generic weight loss substitutes. The company named Maziar Mike Doustdar as the new CEO, effective August 7, 2025.\nIn March 2025, the company announced new plans for a direct-to-consumer offering of its Wegovy weight loss drug. The company established a new pharmacy, called NovoCare, which would charge customers $499 per month for access to the drug, less than half the cost of the drug through other pharmaceutical distribution networks.\nIn September 2025, Novo Nordisk announced that it would reduce its global workforce by 9,000 people, approximately 9% of its total workforce.\nIn October 2025, Novo Nordisk acquired Akero Therapeutics for $5.2 billion. Akero Therapeutics is a clinical stage company focusing on the treatment of\nmetabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis\n(MASH).\nIn November 2025, United States President\nDonald Trump\nannounced a deal with NovoNordisk to lower the costs of Ozempic, including monthly prices of about $245 for injectables and $149 for some oral versions for people on\nMedicare\nand\nMedicaid\nand for those who use his\nTrumpRx\nplatform.\nIn 2026, new obesity GLP-1 pills were launched, with patients getting their hands on them from Danish drugmaker Novo Nordisk.\nProducts\n[\nedit\n]\nNovo Nordisk makes the drug\nsemaglutide\n, used to treat diabetes under the brand names Ozempic and Rybelsus and obesity under the brand name Wegovy.\nNovo Nordisk is also involved with\nhemostasis\nmanagement,\ngrowth hormone\ntherapy, and\nhormone replacement therapy\n. The company makes several drugs under various brand names, including\nLevemir\n,\nTresiba\n,\nNovoLog\n, Novolin R,\nNovoSeven\n,\nNovoEight\n, and\nVictoza\n.\nToxicogenomics\n[\nedit\n]\nNovo Nordisk is involved in government funded collaborative research projects with other industrial and governmental partners. One example in the area of\nnon-clinical\nsafety assessment is the InnoMed PredTox.\nThe company is expanding its activities in joint research projects within the framework of the\nInnovative Medicines Initiative\nof\nEuropean Federation of Pharmaceutical Industries and Associations\nand the\nEuropean Commission\n.\nResearch and pipeline\n[\nedit\n]\nIn 2025, Novo Nordisk tested whether semaglutide helped slow progression for Alzheimer’s disease. However, on Nov. 24, 2025, the company announced that the studies failed to find any effect of the drug on cognition and functioning in people with mild cognitive impairment or with dementia.\nNovo Nordisk was researching\npulmonary\ndelivery systems for diabetic medications, and in the early stages of research into\nautoimmune\nand chronic inflammatory diseases, using technologies such as translational immunology and monoclonal antibodies.\nIn September 2014, the company announced a decision to discontinue all research in inflammatory disorders, including the discontinuation of R&D in anti-\nIL-20\nfor the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis.\nIn September 2018, it was reported that the company would lay off 400 administrative staff, laboratory technicians and scientists, in Denmark and China in order to concentrate  research and development efforts on “transformational biological and technological innovation”.\nControversies\n[\nedit\n]\nIn 2010, Novo Nordisk breached the code of conduct for\nAssociation of the British Pharmaceutical Industry\n(ABPI), by failing to provide information about side-effects of\nVictoza\nand by promoting\nVictoza\nprior to being granted market authorisation.\nIn 2013, Novo Nordisk had to pay back\n3.6\nkr.\nbillion to the\nDanish tax authorities\ndue to transfer mispricing.\nIn March 2013, a debate emerged in which scientists questioned whether the incretin class of diabetic medications – the class to which\nVictoza\nbelongs – had an increased risk of side effects in the pancreas such as pancreatitis and pancreatic cancer. It was concluded that data currently available did not confirm these concerns.\nIn October 2013, batches of NovoMix 30 FlexPen and Penfill insulin were recalled in some European countries as their analysis had shown that a small percentage of the products in these batches did not meet the specifications for insulin strength.\nIn September 2017, Novo Nordisk agreed to pay $58.7 million to end a\nUnited States Department of Justice\nprobe into the lack of FDA disclosure to doctors about the cancer risk for their diabetic drug, Victoza.\nIn March 2023, Novo Nordisk was suspended from the ABPI for a period of two years, for engaging in misleading marketing practices that amounted to "bribing health professionals with inducement to prescribe".\nThis is only the eighth time in the last 40 years that ABPI sanctioned a member organization.\nConsequently, the\nRoyal College of General Practitioners\nand the\nRoyal College of Physicians\nended their corporate partnerships as it would be in breach of their ethical guidance. The Novo Nordisk UK General Manager, Pinder Sahota, chose to resign as President of the ABPI prior to the suspension.\nIn August 2023, one of the first lawsuits was filed alleging that Novo Nordisk’s Ozempic and\nEli Lilly\n’s\nMounjaro\n(tirzepatide) caused severe gastrointestinal side effects, including\ngastroparesis\n.\nThe lawsuit was brought on behalf of Jaclyn Bjorklund, a\nLouisiana\nwoman represented by\nMorgan & Morgan\n, who alleged that she suffered persistent vomiting and other serious injuries after using the drugs and that the manufacturers failed to adequately warn patients and physicians about the risks.\nIn September 2023, the law firm\nMotley Rice\nfiled a lawsuit on behalf of a\nPennsylvania\nwoman who had been taking weight-loss drug Wegovy.\nThe lawsuit alleges that the plaintiff experienced severe stomach cramping, vomiting, and nausea requiring hospitalization, and that Novo Nordisk failed to adequately warn patients about the risk of gastroparesis.\nIn February 2024, the United States Judicial Panel on Multidistrict Litigation\nordered\nthat 55 lawsuits pending in federal courts be consolidated into a multidistrict litigation. The majority of the cases were against Novo Nordisk, but some were brought against Eli Lilly. As of August 2024 there were 235 active Ozempic lawsuits. Following the consolidation, the court appointed a plaintiffs’ leadership committee to coordinate the litigation, which included attorneys from the law firms Susen Law Group, Motley Rice,\nLevin Papantonio\n,  Morgan & Morgan,\nPogust Goodhead\n, and Johnson Becker.\nIn July 2024, Todd Engel, a\nHoward County\nman represented by attorneys from Motley Rice, filed a nine-count complaint in New Jersey state court alleging that Ozempic left him permanently blind.\nEngel claimed that Novo Nordisk failed to warn doctors or consumers about the risk of non-arteritic anterior ischemic optic neuropathy and accused the company of negligent misrepresentation and defective design.\nIn 2024 Novo Nordisk drug pricing in the US has been a target of lawmakers, including Senator\nBernie Sanders\nand the Senate committee\nHealth, Education, Labor and Pensions (HELP)\n. The committee investigation found Novo Nordisk's drug Ozempic priced for $969 per month in the US, compared to $155 in Canada and $59 in Germany. Its weight-loss drug Wegovy is priced for $1,349 per month in the US compared to $140 in Germany and $92 in the UK.\nIn July 2024, US President\nJoe Biden\njoined Sanders in stating "Novo Nordisk and\nEli Lilly\nmust stop ripping off Americans with high drug prices."\nIn September 2024,\nLars Fruergaard Jørgensen\n, CEO at the time was summoned to testify to the\nUS Senate Health, Education, Labor and Pensions Committee\nat a hearing in\nWashington DC\n. During the hearing Senator Sanders reiterated his issues with the pricing of weight loss drug.\nIn 2025, the Irish\nHealth Products Regulatory Authority\nidentified several non-compliances relating to advertising rules in educational and training materials in 2021 and 2022. A May 2021 teaching guide did not feature statements on adverse reactions, precautions and contraindications, while materials in November 2021 and April 2022 were unbalanced and promotional in nature, and consideration was not given to the appropriateness of hiring a trainer to carry out consultations with patients during a pilot training course.\nThat same year, U.S. District Judge Karen Marston issued the first substantive rulings in the multidistrict litigation, dismissing several claims while allowing core allegations - including claims that the manufacturers failed to warn about gastrointestinal risks - to proceed.\nAt that stage, the litigation encompassed more than 2,600 individual lawsuits involving Ozempic, Mounjaro, and related GLP-1 receptor agonist drugs.\nCorporate affairs\n[\nedit\n]\nFinances\n[\nedit\n]\nFor the fiscal year 2024, Novo Nordisk reported earnings of DKK 101 billion (around 14.5 billion\nUSD\n), with an annual revenue of DKK 290.4 billion (around 42.1 billion USD), an increase of 25% over the previous fiscal cycle.\nOwnership\n[\nedit\n]\nNovo Nordisk shares are mostly owned by institutional investors and Novo Holdings A/S.
