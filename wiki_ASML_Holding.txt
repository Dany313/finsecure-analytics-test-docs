Source URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ASML_Holding\n==================================================\n\nDutch photolithography company\nFor others, see\nASML (disambiguation)\n.\nASML Holding N.V.\n(originally standing for\nAdvanced Semiconductor Materials Lithography\n) is a Dutch\nmultinational corporation\nthat develops and manufactures\nphotolithography\nmachines which are used to produce\nintegrated circuits\n. It is the largest supplier for the\nsemiconductor industry\n, as well as the most advanced producer of\nextreme ultraviolet lithography\n(EUV) machines that are required to manufacture the most advanced chips.\nAs of January\n2026\n, its market capitalization was approximately $527 billion, making it Europe's largest technology company and one of its most valuable firms overall.\nASML was founded in 1984 as a\njoint venture\nbetween the Dutch technology companies\nPhilips\nand\nASM International\n; it became an independent publicly traded entity four years later. Having been established to complete development of Philips' previously in-house project to produce their own lithography machine, ASML initially had no market-ready products. Furthermore, ASML's first product, the PAS 2000, was a commercial and technical failure; however, the company did attain commercial success during the early 1990s with the PAS 5500. During 1997, ASML began studying the potential shift to EUV. By 2002, the company had become the largest supplier of lithography machines. While sales were negatively impacted by the start of the\nGreat Recession\nin 2008, ASML had effectively recovered by 2011. In July 2012,\nIntel\npurchased a 15 percent stake in ASML in exchange for $4.1 billion;\nSamsung\nand\nTSMC\nalso bought stakes in the firm under a co-investment program. ASML completed development of its EUV machines in the late 2010s. During the 2010s and 2020s, both the US and Dutch governments imposed new oversight and restrictions on each individual sale of ASML technology to China.\nASML's corporate headquarters is in\nVeldhoven\n, just outside the city of\nEindhoven\nin the Netherlands, and is the location for research, development, manufacturing, and assembly. ASML employs more than 42,000 people\nfrom 143 nationalities and relies on a network of nearly 5,000 tier 1 suppliers.\nASML has a worldwide customer base and over 60 service points in 16 countries.\nIt has offices in the Netherlands, the United States, Belgium, France, Germany, Ireland, Israel, Italy, the United Kingdom, China, Hong Kong, Japan, South Korea, Malaysia, Singapore, and Taiwan.\nThe company is listed on both the\nEuronext Amsterdam\nand\nNasdaq\nstock exchanges, as ASML. It is also a component of the\nEuro Stoxx 50\nand\nNasdaq-100\n.\nHistory\n[\nedit\n]\nBackground\n[\nedit\n]\nASML can be traced back to the 1970s and the Dutch electronics company\nPhilips\n. The firm, having observed that the wider electronics industry had been struggling to shrink features without losing accuracy or letting contamination/flaws increase, decided to develop its own prototype lithography machines, harnessing Philips' established knowledge in the fields of optics and precision mechanics.\nBy the early 1980s, Philips had projected that considerable additional investment would be necessary to complete its development, but this need came at a time in which the company was trying to reduce expenditure. Accordingly, in 1984, the company elected to\nspin-off\nits lithography machine activities into a\njoint venture\nwith another Dutch firm,\nASM International\n, which specialised in the supply of equipment to the semiconductor industry.\nThe new entity used a different business strategy to established competitors;\nlacking the capital, time, and expertise to realistically pursue\nvertical integration\n, instead, the joint venture opted to\noutsource\nvarious major components, including\nmotors\nand\noptics\n, to other parties so that it could dedicate its resources and attention on the assembly and optimisation of the final machine. This decision shaped the machine as well, which adopted a\nmodular design\nand used clearly defined subsystems.\nEarly on, ASML struggled to secure business, having no market share and no brand recognition at the start of its operations. Furthermore, the firm's first product, the PAS 2000, was a commercial and technical failure, having used oil pressure (instead of electric motors) to move the wafer during exposure, which had a tendency to leak; future lithograph machines made by ASML would use conventional electric motors instead.\nIndependence and commercial breakout\n[\nedit\n]\nBy the start of 1988, Philips was reportedly considered shutting down the joint venture entirely after ASM International, however, sufficient support was present on Philip's board to continue with operations, giving ASML the time to develop another lithography machine, the PAS 5500.\nAccordingly, in 1988, ASML became an independent publicly traded entity; that same year, it first adopted the name ASML,\nwhich is its official name and not an abbreviation.\nIn 1991, ASML released the PAS 5500, which quickly became the company's first commercial breakout.\nIn contrast to the more precise contemporary lithography machines from competing firm\nNikon\n, the modular design used by ASML meant that its machines could be fixed quickly on site, reducing downtime and making it possible to extend the machine’s operating life. The serviceability advantage was reportedly the key factor that led to\nIBM\nordering the PAS 5500 over its Japanese counterparts.\nThe first company to operate the PAS 5500 was\nMicron Technology\n, one of the world's largest producers of computer memory and storage; it became ASML's largest customer for a time.\nThe success of the PAS 5500 line propelled ASML into a period of intense competition with both\nCanon\nand Nikon, who were the leaders of the lithography market at the time.\nIn 1997, ASML began studying a shift to using extreme ultraviolet.\nTwo years later, it joined a consortium, which included\nIntel\nand two other U.S. chipmakers, in order to exploit fundamental research conducted by the\nUS Department of Energy\n. Because the\nCooperative Research and Development Agreement (CRADA)\nit operates under is funded by the US government, licensing must be approved by\nCongress\n.\nFurthermore, in return for being permitted to join this consortium, ASML committed to the establishment of a US-based research center and to source 55 percent of components for all machines sold in the US from American suppliers.\nASML collaborated with various manufacturers to obtain mirrors, including the Belgian firms\nIMEC\nand\nSematech\nas well as Germany's\nCarl Zeiss\n.\n2000s\n[\nedit\n]\nIn 2001, ASML acquired the US-based lithography equipment manufacturer Silicon Valley Group (SVG) after it had encountered liquidity issues,\nThe SVG acquisition, which has also been licensed for EUV research results, involved ASML in an arrangement to supply 193 nm scanners to Intel.\nIn 2002, it became the largest supplier of photolithography systems.\nAided by the adoption of the dual-stage TWINSCAN architecture to reduce idle time, ASML machines could produce more chips per hour than any competitor's products; this competitive edge was so strong that the company could charge higher prices for their machines and still grow its market share. By the end of the decade, it had secured two thirds of the global lithography market and had become the dominant supplier for the growing\nsmartphone\nmarket.\nDuring December 2006, ASML announced the acquisition of rival firm BRION Technologies in exchange for $270 million;\nthis transaction into direction competition with the major EDA vendors who had similar products -\nCadence Design Systems\n,\nSynopsys\n, and\nMentor Graphics\n.\nDuring late 2008, following the start of the\nGreat Recession\n, ASML experienced a large drop in sales, which led management to cut the workforce by about 1000 worldwide, mainly at its headquarters in the Netherlands as well its Connecticut manufacturing plant;\nadditionally, the firm applied for support from the Dutch national unemployment fund to prevent even larger layoffs.\nASML had recovered by 2011, reporting record-high revenue for that year.\n2010s\n[\nedit\n]\nDuring the early 2010s, ASML sharply increased its annual R&D budget, rising from just under $500 million in 2010 to $1 billion in 2015.\nWhile this expenditure was necessary in order to complete the development of its next generation EUV machines, financing such amounts was not straightforward. To achieve this, ASML launched a co-investment program in 2012, under which 23 percent of the company was sold to its three largest customers: Intel,\nTSMC\nand\nSamsung\n.\nIntel's investment into $4.1 billion into ASML, made in July 2012, gave it a 15 percent stake in the company; this move was stated to accelerate Intel's transition from 300\nmm to\n450 mm wafers\nas well as support further development of EUV lithography.\nThat same month, ASML offered another 10 percent of its shares to other companies.\nBeyond simply generating additional funds, these new stakeholders were incentivised to participate in ASML's research efforts. TSMC in particular worked closely with ASML to develop and troubleshoot its EUV machines, being keen to use them to manufacture their own products.\nAs part of their EUV strategy, ASML announced the acquisition of DUV and EUV sources manufacturer\nCymer\nin exchange for $2.55 billion in October 2012;\nthis transaction was completed in May 2013.\nCymer's technology was viewed as being useful to developing ASML's EUV machines.\nIn November 2013, ASML paused development of 450\nmm lithography equipment, citing uncertain timing of chipmaker demand.\nIn early 2015, ASML reported an instance of\nintellectual property theft\n; allegedly a number of employees had been found stealing confidential data from its\nSilicon Valley\nsoftware subsidiary that develops software for machine optimization.\nThree years later, ASML was awarded $223 million in damages pertaining to this incident, as well as securing the offending company's own intellectual property.\nASML employees have often been viewed as highly desirable recruitment targets for other tech companies.\nIn June 2016, ASML announced their plans to acquire Taiwan-based Hermes Microvision Inc. in exchange for about $3.1 billion; it was reported that this transaction would secure technology for creating smaller and more advanced\nsemiconductors\n.\nIn 2018, the\nTrump administration\ntried to block the sale of ASML technology to China.\nThis move, which has been viewed as part of a wider "technological cold war" between the US and China, may have contributed to the\n2020–2023 global chip shortage\nand impacted the business opportunities available to ASML.\n2020s\n[\nedit\n]\nThroughout the late 2010s and early 2020s, ASML experienced a period of steady revenue growth, rising from $13 billion in 2018 up to $35 billion in 2024; this has been largely credited to the firm's EUV machines, which have received a significant surge in demand during this period, driven by modern electronics' increasing complexity and performance requirements.\nIn July 2020, ASML stated that it had acquired the German\noptical glassmaking\nfirm Berliner Glas Group, which will help to satisfy the increasing need for components for its EUV systems; at the time of this acquisition, ASML's outstanding orders had reportedly risen to €10 billion.\nIn July 2021, European Commissioner\nThierry Breton\n, visited ASML and announced a goal of at least 20 percent of global production of semiconductors in Europe by 2030, and support via a European Alliance on semiconductors.\nAfter reporting earnings in July 2021, the company said they had a near monopoly for machines used by TSMC and Samsung to make the advanced chips.\nIn February 2023, ASML claimed that a former worker in China "allegedly" stole information about the company's technology. This was not the first time that ASML was allegedly linked with an intellectual property breach connected to China. In its 2021\nannual report\n, ASML mentioned that\nDongfang Jingyuan Electron Limited\n"was actively marketing products in China that could potentially infringe on ASML's IP rights."\nAt the time, the\nUnited States Department of Commerce\nexpressed concern about\neconomic espionage\nagainst ASML.\nIn October 2023, Dutch newspaper\nNRC Handelsblad\nreported that the former employee who "allegedly" stole data about ASML's technology subsequently went to work for\nHuawei\n.\nIn March 2023, the Dutch government placed restrictions on chip equipment exports in order to protect national security. This measure affected ASML as one of the most important companies in the global microchip supply chain.\nExport license requirements came into effect in September 2023.\nThree months later, the Netherlands' Institute for Human Rights ruled that despite the country's\nconstitution\nprohibiting discrimination based on nationality, ASML was allowed to reject job applications from residents of countries subject to sanctions under the U.S.\nExport Administration Regulations\n(such as China, Cuba, Iran, North Korea, and Syria) in order to remain compliant with U.S. law.\nIn January 2024, the Dutch government placed further restrictions on the shipment of some advanced chip-making equipment to China.\nOn 6 September 2024, the Dutch government tightened export controls on certain ASML chipmaking equipment, aligning its policy with U.S. restrictions to limit China's access to advanced technology amid safety and geopolitical concerns.\nThese restrictions were expanded for tighter export controls in January 2025, with ASML required to apply for export licenses with the Dutch government instead of the U.S.\nIn December 2025,\nReuters\nreported that China had secretly built a prototype EUV machine in\nShenzhen\nwith the assistance of former ASML engineers, with the machine expected to produce working chips between 2028 and 2030.\nProducts\n[\nedit\n]\nA diagonally cut ASML lens\nASML produces the\nphotolithography\nmachines used in the production of integrated circuits, called "scanners". In these machines, patterns are optically imaged onto a silicon\nwafer\nthat is covered with a film of light-sensitive material (\nphotoresist\n). This procedure is repeated dozens of times on a single wafer. The photoresist is then further processed to create the actual electronic circuits on the silicon. The optical imaging that ASML's machines deal with is used in the fabrication of nearly all integrated circuits.\nAs of 2025\n, ASML had 83% percent of the worldwide sales of lithography machines.\nASML's earlier competition consisted of\nUltratech\n,\nCanon\n,\nNikon\n,\nMKS Instruments\n,\nLam Research\nand\nCadence Design Systems\n.\nImmersion lithography\n[\nedit\n]\nSince\nimmersion lithography\nwas first proposed by\nBurn-Jeng Lin\nin the 1970s,\nASML cooperated with\nTSMC\n. In 2004, TSMC began commercial production of\n90 nanometer\nsemiconductor nodes\nusing ASML immersion lithography.\nBy  2011, their high-end TWINSCAN NXT:1950i system was used for producing features down to 32\nnanometres\nat up to 200 wafers per hour,\nusing a water\nimmersion lens\nand an\nargon fluoride laser\nthat produces light at a wavelength of 193\nnm. By 2011, an average lithography machine cost\n€\n27 million\n.\nDUV lithography\n[\nedit\n]\nDeep ultraviolet lithography (DUV) devices from ASML use ultraviolet light to print the tiny features that form the microchip's structure.\nEUV lithography\n[\nedit\n]\nExtreme ultraviolet lithography\n(EUV) is a critical technology used to create the smallest and most complex chip designs. ASML holds a near-monopoly in the EUV market. The company's machines are capable of etching patterns as small as 8 nanometers, 1/10000 the width of a human hair.\nEUV machines produce light in the 13.5\nnm\nwavelength\nrange by focusing a high-energy\nlaser\non microscopic droplets of molten\ntin\nto produce a\nplasma\n, which then emits EUV light. The light is bounced off\nZeiss\nmirrors onto the surface of a silicon wafer to form the designs for the chip.\nIn 2009, the\nIMEC\nresearch center in Belgium produced the world's first functional 22\nnm\nCMOS\nStatic random-access memory\nmemory cells with a prototype EUV lithography machine.\nAfter decades of development, ASML shipped the first production EUV machine in either 2011\nor 2013.\nAs of 2022\n, ASML has shipped around 140 EUV systems.\nASML's best-selling EUV product has been the TWINSCAN NXE:3600D, which costs up to $200 million.\nThe machine weighs 180 tons and needs three\nBoeing 747s\nto transport.\nASML is working on the next generation of EUV systems, with the first shipments for R&D shipped to\nIntel\nin December 2023, and TSMC in late 2024.\nThe platform is designated\nHigh-NA\nas it increases the\nnumerical aperture\n(NA) from 0.33 to 0.55.\neach system costs approximately $370 million.\nSponsorships\n[\nedit\n]\nASML became a sponsor of the\nPSV\nfootball club in 2019 together with\nPhilips\n,\nVDL Groep\n,\nRoyal Swinkels Family Brewers\nand\nJumbo Supermarkets\nfrom the Brainport region.\nTogether they run various initiatives like soccer\ntraining camps\nfor school children, the development of interactive programs for teaching, assisting community members in need or who are new to the region, as well as supporting a vitality program that is online.\nASML was the title partner of the\nEindhoven marathon\nfrom 2022 - 2024.\nAwards\n[\nedit\n]\nThe\nIEEE\nSpectrum Emerging Technology 2018 Award was given to ASML for its Extreme Ultraviolet Lithography system. ASML is currently the top supplier of photolithography systems to the semiconductor industry.\nIn the category for Popular Prize, Vadim Banine received the 2018 European Inventor Award for shaping the future of microchip manufacturing.\nThe largest independent microelectronics research center in Europe is called\nIMEC\n(Interuniversity Micro Electronics Centre). Martin van den Brink of ASML was given the 2019 IMEC Lifetime of Innovation Award.\nThe Netherlands Association for Investor Relations (NEVIR), where listed companies and professionals in investor relations had the chance to be recognized for their outstanding work in the sector, presented awards to ASML in the categories: "Best Company in the field of Investor Relations" during the Dutch IR Awards 2019 annual award ceremony.\nASML received the\nSEMI\nAmericas Award at the 2020 edition of the microelectronics conference SEMICON West for its collaborative approach to\nextreme ultraviolet lithography\n(EUV), which helped it become commercially viable and opened the door to new technological possibilities.\nThe Intel Preferred Quality Supplier (PQS) Award for 2020 was awarded to ASML. ASML has attained a level of performance that continuously surpasses Intel's expectations for the commitment to continual quality improvement.\nThe Dutch Innovation Prize 2021 was awarded to ASML at the 4th National BID AVROTROS Innovation Dinner held at Kasteel Wittenburg in\nWassenaar\n.\nASML received the first CoSta Award for the most successful and impactful innovative partnership between a corporate company and a startup.\nReferences\n[\nedit\n]\n1\n2\n3\n4\n5\n6\n7\n"ASML 2025 Annual Report (Form 20-F)"\n.
