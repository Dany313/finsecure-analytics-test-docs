The company became influential in the\nrise of personal computers\nthrough software like\nWindows\nand has since expanded into areas such as Internet services,\ncloud computing\n,\nartificial intelligence\n, video gaming, and more. A\nBig Tech\ncompany, Microsoft is the\nlargest software company by revenue\n, one of the\nmost valuable public companies\n, and one of the\nmost valuable brands\nglobally.\nFounded in 1975 by\nBill Gates\nand\nPaul Allen\nto market\nBASIC interpreters\nfor the\nAltair 8800\n, Microsoft rose to dominate the PC operating system market with\nMS-DOS\nin the mid-1980s, followed by Windows. The company's 1986\ninitial public offering\n(IPO) and subsequent rise in its share price created three billionaires and an estimated 12,000\nmillionaires among Microsoft employees. Since the 1990s, it has increasingly diversified its business.\nSteve Ballmer\nreplaced Gates as CEO in 2000. He oversaw the then-largest of\nMicrosoft's corporate acquisitions\nin\nSkype Technologies\nin 2011, an increased focus on hardware that led to its first in-house PC line—the\nSurface\n—in 2012, and the formation of\nMicrosoft Mobile\nthrough\nNokia\n. Since\nSatya Nadella\nbecame CEO in 2014, the company has changed focus towards cloud computing and acquired\nLinkedIn\nin 2016. Under his direction, the company has expanded its video gaming business to support the\nXbox\nbrand, establishing the\nMicrosoft Gaming\ndivision in 2022 and\nacquiring Activision Blizzard\nin 2023.\nMicrosoft has been dominant in the\nIBM PC–compatible\noperating system and office software suite markets since the 1990s. Its best-known\nsoftware products\nare the Windows line of\noperating systems\nand the\nMicrosoft Office\nand\nMicrosoft 365\nsuite of productivity applications, which most notably include the\nWord\nword processor,\nExcel\nspreadsheet editor, and\nPowerPoint\npresentation program. Its flagship\nhardware products\nare the Surface lineup of PCs and the Xbox brand of video game consoles, the latter including the\nXbox network\n. The company also provides a range of consumer Internet services such as\nBing\nweb search, the\nMSN\nweb portal, the\nOutlook.com\n(Hotmail) email service, and the\nMicrosoft Store\n. In the enterprise and development fields, Microsoft most notably provides the\nAzure\ncloud computing platform,\nMicrosoft SQL Server\ndatabase software, and\nVisual Studio\n.\nMicrosoft became the third publicly traded U.S. company to be\nvalued at over $1 trillion\nin April 2019.\nIt has been criticized\nfor\nmonopolistic practices\n, and the company's software received criticism for problems with\nease of use\n,\nrobustness\n, and\nsecurity\n.
